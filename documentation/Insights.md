# Insights
**Key Findings:**  
Revenue trends across years
Revenue fluctuated significantly between 2010 and 2017, with notable peaks in 2012 (£32M) and troughs in 2011 (£11M) and 2015–2016 (£12M). These variations suggest inconsistent campaign timing, uneven product performance, and potential gaps in market coverage. The strong rebound in 2012 indicates that targeted interventions or high‑performing product cycles can materially shift annual outcomes.
Regional profitability performance
Sub‑Saharan Africa delivered the highest total profit (£12.2M), driven by strong unit sales (183K) and balanced channel performance. Europe followed closely (£11.1M), supported by a healthy mix of online and offline sales. Regions such as Central America and North America showed lower profit contributions, reflecting smaller market sizes or weaker product/channel alignment. Australia and Oceania demonstrated strong profitability relative to unit volume, indicating higher margins or more efficient operations.
Channel profitability and contribution
Online sales contributed 56.42% of total profit, outperforming offline channels at 43.58%. This suggests that digital channels are more efficient, scalable, or better aligned with customer purchasing behaviour. Offline channels still play a significant role, especially in regions like Sub‑Saharan Africa and Europe, but the higher online share highlights opportunities to expand digital campaigns and optimise e‑commerce operations.
Unit sales distribution
Unit sales were heavily concentrated in Sub‑Saharan Africa (183K) and Europe (98K), indicating strong demand and established customer bases. Regions with lower unit volumes, such as North America (19K), may require targeted marketing, product repositioning, or improved channel strategies. The alignment between high unit sales and high profit in key regions reinforces the importance of maintaining strong supply chains and regional focus.
Operational and strategic implications
The dominance of online profitability suggests that investment in digital channels—campaign optimisation, targeted ads, and improved customer journeys—could yield higher returns. Regions with strong unit sales but moderate profit may benefit from margin optimisation or product mix adjustments. The volatility in annual revenue highlights the need for consistent campaign scheduling, better forecasting, and KPI‑driven performance management. Strengthening regional strategies and aligning product offerings with local demand can further stabilise revenue and improve long‑term growth.
**Recommendations:**  
-Stabilise annual revenue through consistent campaign scheduling — the large swings between 2010–2017 indicate inconsistent marketing cadence. A structured campaign calendar with quarterly anchor campaigns would reduce volatility.
-Increase investment in online channels — online contributes 56.42% of total profit and outperforms offline in most regions. Expanding digital campaigns, improving targeting, and optimising online funnels will increase ROI.
-Strengthen regional strategies for high‑performing markets — Sub‑Saharan Africa and Europe generate the highest profit and unit sales. Prioritising these regions with tailored promotions, product bundles, and retention strategies will maximise returns.
-Improve product mix and margin optimisation — regions like Australia and Oceania show strong profit relative to units sold, suggesting high‑margin products. Expanding these product lines or replicating pricing strategies in other regions can lift profitability.
-Target underperforming regions with focused interventions — North America and Central America show low unit volumes and profit. Market research, channel realignment, or repositioning campaigns may be needed to unlock growth.
-Enhance forecasting and KPI monitoring — revenue volatility and uneven regional performance highlight the need for stronger forecasting models and continuous KPI tracking to support proactive decision‑making.
**Business Impact:**  
-More predictable revenue performance — consistent campaign planning and improved forecasting reduce volatility and support long‑term financial stability.
-Higher profitability through channel optimisation — shifting budget toward high‑performing online channels increases margins and reduces acquisition costs.
-Stronger regional contribution — focusing on high‑value regions amplifies existing strengths, while targeted interventions in weaker regions unlock new revenue opportunities.
-Improved operational efficiency — optimising product mix and aligning supply with demand reduces waste, improves margins, and enhances customer satisfaction.
-More strategic, data‑driven decisions — KPI‑driven insights enable leadership to act earlier, allocate resources more effectively, and respond to market changes with greater precision.
